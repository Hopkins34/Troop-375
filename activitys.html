Activitys

Troop 375 offers a variety of activities to promote leadership, teamwork, and personal growth. Scouts participate in meetings, outdoor adventures, service projects, and merit badge opportunities. From camping trips to community service, each event helps scouts develop new skills and create lasting memories. Stay updated on upcoming events and get involved with Troop 375!

High Adventure

Sea Base

In June of 2024, Troop 375 had the exciting opportunity to visit Sea Base, a premier Scouting destination, for an unforgettable adventure. Our scouts participated in a scuba diving expedition, exploring the vibrant underwater world. With expert instructors guiding the way, the troop dove into crystal-clear waters, discovering marine life and gaining valuable skills in diving. This experience not only deepened our scouts’ appreciation for the ocean but also helped them grow as individuals, building confidence and teamwork. It was an amazing trip that combined adventure, learning, and fun for all involved.

Northern Tier

Three years ago, Troop 375 had the opportunity to embark on an unforgettable adventure at Northern Tier, a renowned high-adventure base in the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness. Our scouts spent several days canoeing through pristine lakes, portaging between them, and camping under the stars in the remote wilderness. The trip tested their outdoor skills, resilience, and teamwork while allowing them to experience the beauty of nature in its purest form. It was a challenging yet rewarding experience that helped build lasting bonds and fostered a deeper appreciation for the great outdoors.

Philmont

Two years ago, Troop 375 had the incredible opportunity to attend Philmont Scout Ranch, where our scouts embarked on a high-adventure trek through the rugged mountains of New Mexico. The trip included hiking, backpacking, and learning important outdoor skills while enjoying breathtaking scenery. It was an unforgettable experience that challenged our scouts physically and mentally, fostering leadership and teamwork. In addition to Philmont, our troop also participates in the National Youth Leadership Training (NYLT), where scouts develop leadership skills through hands-on activities, team-building exercises, and engaging lessons. Both experiences have had a lasting impact, empowering our scouts to grow as leaders and outdoor enthusiasts.

Community Events

Tree Lot

The Troop 375 Tree Lot is one of our most cherished traditions and key fundraisers. Each holiday season, our scouts and families come together to sell fresh, high-quality Christmas trees to the community. This hands-on experience teaches our scouts valuable skills in customer service, teamwork, and responsibility. The tree lot is more than a fundraiser—it’s a chance to spread holiday cheer, connect with our neighbors, and support troop activities throughout the year. By purchasing a tree from our lot, you’re not only bringing home a symbol of the season but also supporting the growth and development of local youth in Scouting.

Cave Box

Troop 375 is excited to showcase our new cave box, a creative and engaging feature for fundraising events like the Sugar Maple Festival. Designed and built by our scouts, the cave box offers a fun and interactive experience for participants, challenging them to navigate through a dark and winding maze. It’s a highlight at community events, drawing in visitors of all ages and helping the troop raise funds for our activities and adventures. The cave box reflects the ingenuity and hard work of our scouts while providing a unique way to connect with the community and support Scouting.

Outdoors

Hiking

Troop 375 takes pride in its strong tradition of hiking, offering scouts the chance to explore nature while building fitness, resilience, and camaraderie. From local trails to more challenging treks, our hikes are tailored to provide both adventure and skill-building opportunities. Scouts learn navigation, Leave No Trace principles, and teamwork as they tackle varied terrains and enjoy the beauty of the outdoors. Whether it’s a short day hike or part of a longer camping trip, hiking is a cornerstone of our program, helping scouts develop a lifelong appreciation for nature and outdoor exploration.

Canoeing

Canoeing is a favorite activity for Troop 375, offering scouts the chance to explore waterways while building essential skills and teamwork. Whether paddling on calm lakes or navigating flowing rivers, our canoeing trips provide both adventure and education. Scouts learn paddling techniques, water safety, and how to work together to steer and power their canoes. These experiences also foster a connection to nature as they explore scenic landscapes and wildlife. Canoeing is not just about adventure—it’s about developing confidence, resilience, and a deeper appreciation for the outdoors.
